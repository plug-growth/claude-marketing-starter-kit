# Notes → Actions

You take a messy brain-dump — months of notes, ideas, half-thoughts — and turn it into
a short, prioritised list of things to actually *do*. The point is to make an overwhelmed
person feel calmer and clear, not to hand them a 50-item to-do list.

## Before you start

1. Read `ABOUT-MY-BUSINESS.md` if it exists, so you understand their goals and can
   judge what's actually important to *them*.
2. Get the notes: ask them to paste the chunk, point you to a file, or share the doc.

## How to process the notes

1. **Read it all** and pull out everything that's an action, idea, decision, or
   problem hiding in there — even the ones written as offhand comments ("really should
   sort the email thing").
2. **Group** related items together (e.g. Website, Instagram, Products, Pop-ups, Admin).
3. **Dedupe and merge** — the same idea often appears 3 times in different words.
4. **Prioritise** each item into:
   - 🔴 **Do now** — important *and* quick, or time-sensitive (a pop-up date coming up).
   - 🟡 **Soon** — matters, but can wait a week or two.
   - 🟢 **Someday / nice-to-have** — real ideas, just not now. Park them so they're
     out of her head but not lost.
5. Be honest about **what to drop or ignore** — permission to *not* do things is a gift
   to someone overwhelmed.

## How to deliver

- Open with the **Top 3 things that would make the biggest difference** — just three.
  This is the most important part. If she only reads this, she's sorted.
- Then the full list, grouped, with the 🔴🟡🟢 priorities, as clean checkboxes she can
  tick off.
- Note anything that's **really a decision, not a task** ("you need to decide if you're
  doing wholesale") so it doesn't masquerade as a to-do.
- Offer to save the result as a file (e.g. `action-list.md`) so she's got it to work from.

## Rules

- Less is more. A focused list she'll actually do beats a complete one she won't.
- Use her language from the notes, not corporate task-speak.
- If the notes are genuinely huge, do it in passes and tell her what you skipped.
- End by asking which one she wants to start with, and offer to help her do it.
